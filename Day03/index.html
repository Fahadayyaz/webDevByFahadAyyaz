Ada Lovelace

Ada Lovelace was a brilliant mathematician and is widely celebrated as the world’s first computer programmer.
In the 1840s, she wrote the first algorithm intended to be carried out by a machine, Charles Babbage's Analytical Engine.
Beyond just calculations, she visionarily realized that computers could be used to create music and art,
a concept that was far ahead of her time.
